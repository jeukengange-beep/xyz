step 1 · market research & product clarity

Problem framing

Core tension

Douala & Yaoundé residents want nutritious meals that arrive when promised, yet existing delivery apps focus on à-la-carte convenience, not predictability.

  • Kitchen space is shrinking while workdays extend beyond 6pm.
  • Parents & teams spend excess time coordinating meals.

Opportunity hypothesis

XYZ Express can own the “set-and-forget” weekly meal routine by blending subscription logistics with cultural menus and flexible billing.

The next slides detail how we will validate demand before scaling.

Defining the product type

Options evaluated

TypeDescriptionRisks
Physical subscriptionPre-cooked meals delivered daily from central kitchens.High capex & limited menu customization.
Digital-onlyMeal planning app with partner restaurants fulfilling orders.No control over quality or service level.
Hybrid (selected)XYZ-owned menus, prep hubs, and scheduled delivery; digital portal for plan management.Requires tightly run operations + concierge onboarding.

Why hybrid wins

  • Guarantees delivery windows while still offering digital plan controls.
  • Enables upsells (family dinners, office snacks) without extra rider loops.
  • Supports flexible billing (weekly mobile money, monthly invoice).

Decision gate: secure two micro-fulfillment hubs and pre-vetted riders before launch.

Walk the audience through the progression. Highlight what operational levers unlock each improvement.

Why this launch, why now?

  • Urban cores add +6% new renters yearly — kitchens are shrinking, delivery spend +28% YoY.
  • Current delivery players miss lunch SLAs 1-in-3 times during peak traffic.
  • Surveyed 212 residents: 64% would prepay weekly if time slots & diet choices were guaranteed.
Underline that we validated pain via secondary data and our own pulse survey last month.

Barriers & enablers to subscription

Adoption barriers

  • Budget predictability — fear of paying upfront without tasting.
  • Trust — concerns about hygiene and rider professionalism.
  • Variety — repetitive menus cause subscription fatigue.

How we address them

  • Weekly mobile money billing + pause button from onboarding.
  • Chef credentials, kitchen tours, real-time delivery map.
  • Seasonal rotations, cultural specials, family add-ons.

Measured by: trial-to-subscription conversion ≥ 60% and churn intent < 20% at concierge exit interviews.

Persona · Jeanne (L'Étudiante Pressée)

  • 24 ans, infirmière stagiaire à Douala, coloc avec 2 amies.
  • Objectif: manger équilibré < 70 000 FCFA/mois sans cuisiner après les gardes.
  • Déclencheur: reprise de stage intensif + besoin de repas sûrs à midi.
  • Objections: peur d'engagement trop long, méfiance vis-à-vis des riders.

Ce que nous lui offrons

  • Plan « Étudiante Flex » : livraison garantie avant 11h45, 3 pauses gratuites / semestre.
  • Programme parrainage colocataire → crédit repas.
  • Notifications WhatsApp + suivi de calories.

Succès: 80% des étudiantes pilotes renouvellent après 4 semaines.

Persona · Daniel (Le Responsable Ops)

  • Responsable support client, 18 collaborateurs en rotation à Yaoundé.
  • Objectif: repas ponctuels, facture unique, menus adaptés aux shifts.
  • Déclencheur: plainte récurrente sur retards de traiteurs actuels.
  • Objections: qualité constante, preuve de conformité hygiène.

Ce que nous lui offrons

  • Portail « Team Pulse » : menus publiés 7 jours à l'avance, suivi SLA en direct.
  • Facturation mensuelle + tableau export CSV.
  • Compte manager dédié & sessions dégustation trimestrielles.

Succès: signer 5 comptes pilotes → 150 repas quotidiens.
