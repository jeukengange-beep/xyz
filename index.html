step 1 · market research & product clarity

Problem framing

Core tension

Douala & Yaounde residents want nutritious meals that arrive when promised, yet existing delivery apps focus on a-la-carte convenience, not predictability.

  • Kitchen space is shrinking while workdays extend beyond 6pm.
  • Parents & teams spend excess time coordinating meals.

Opportunity hypothesis

XYZ Express can own the “set-and-forget” weekly meal routine by blending subscription logistics with cultural menus and flexible billing.

The next slides detail how we will validate demand before scaling.

Defining the product type

Options evaluated

TypeDescriptionRisks
Physical subscriptionPre-cooked meals delivered daily from central kitchens.High capex & limited menu customization.
Digital-onlyMeal planning app with partner restaurants fulfilling orders.No control over quality or service level.
Hybrid (selected)XYZ-owned menus, prep hubs, and scheduled delivery; digital portal for plan management.Requires tightly run operations + concierge onboarding.

Why hybrid wins

  • Guarantees delivery windows while still offering digital plan controls.
  • Enables upsells (family dinners, office snacks) without extra rider loops.
  • Supports flexible billing (weekly mobile money, monthly invoice).

Decision gate: secure two micro-fulfillment hubs and pre-vetted riders before launch.

Demand validation research plan

Primary methods

  • 12 user interviews — Student nurses, young parents, SMB ops leads.
    Outcome: priority meal jobs-to-be-done & willingness-to-pay bands.
  • Concierge MVP (WhatsApp) — 4-week pilot, manual routing.
    Outcome: track readiness to prepay; success if ≥70% complete 2 cycles.

Secondary methods

  • Desk research — FAO, GSMA, McKinsey food delivery reports.
    Outcome: quantify urban meal spend & growth forecasts.
  • Competitor audit — Tchop et Yamo, K My Choice, Glovo.
    Outcome: map pricing, service gaps, loyalty levers.
Highlight that validation combines live touchpoints with market data, fulfilling the AltSchool prompt: two primary and two secondary methods.

Competitive snapshot

Tchop & Yamo

  • Focuses on Cameroonian comfort meals and seasonal menus.
  • Strong social engagement but inconsistent delivery windows.
  • Opportunity: Emphasize XYZ's scheduled slots and concierge care.

K.My Choice

  • Promotes office catering with broad buffet options.
  • Limited subscription messaging; relies on ad-hoc ordering.
  • Opportunity: Position XYZ as the predictable weekly partner with data-rich reporting.

Both competitors reinforce demand for culturally resonant meals while leaving a gap for reliability and subscription-first experiences.

Barriers & enablers to subscription

Adoption barriers

  • Budget predictability — fear of paying upfront without tasting.
  • Trust — concerns about hygiene and rider professionalism.
  • Variety — repetitive menus cause subscription fatigue.

How we address them

  • Weekly mobile money billing + pause button from onboarding.
  • Chef credentials, kitchen tours, real-time delivery map.
  • Seasonal rotations, cultural specials, family add-ons.

Measured by: trial-to-subscription conversion ≥ 60% and churn intent < 20% at concierge exit interviews.

Persona · Jeanne — Focused Nursing Intern

  • Goal: Eat balanced meals under 70,000 FCFA per month without cooking after evening shifts.
  • Trigger: Intensive hospital rotation resumes and she needs reliable midday meals.
  • Objections: Worries about long commitments and rider professionalism.

How XYZ serves Jeanne

  • Student Flex plan — guaranteed delivery by 11:45 a.m. with three pause credits per semester.
  • Roommate referral — earns meal credits when friends subscribe.
  • WhatsApp concierge — reminders, nutrition snapshots, and swap requests.

Success signal: ≥80% of student pilots renew after four weeks.

Persona · Daniel — Operations Lead

  • Heads customer support for an 18-person team in Yaounde with rotating shifts.
  • Goal: Reliable meals, a single invoice, and menus that match day and night crews.
  • Trigger: Escalating complaints about late caterers impacting service levels.
  • Objections: Needs proof of consistent quality and hygiene compliance.

How XYZ serves Daniel

  • Team Pulse portal — menus published seven days ahead with live SLA tracking.
  • Consolidated billing — monthly invoice plus CSV export for finance.
  • Dedicated account manager — quarterly tasting sessions and onboarding toolkit.

Success signal: Sign five pilot accounts that total 150 daily meals.

step 3 · positioning & messaging

Positioning statement

Statement

For busy households and growth-focused teams in Douala & Yaounde
Who need reliable, nutritious meals without daily coordination,
XYZ Express is a hybrid meal subscription that guarantees scheduled delivery windows and culturally relevant menus.
Unlike on-demand apps or single-chef services,
we manage prep-to-door operations with certified hubs, vetted riders, and a concierge onboarding squad.

Reasons to believe

  • Two micro-fulfillment hubs ready with HACCP-compliant processes.
  • 15 riders pre-contracted with incentive-based SLA.
  • Nutritionist-designed 6-week rotation featuring local classics.

Value propositions

Individuals & families

  • Scheduled deliveries for every critical window (lunch and dinner).
  • Flexible plans: Student Flex, Active Duo, Weekend Family bundles.
  • Transparent nutrition data plus vegetarian and gluten-free paths.

Businesses & partners

  • Single invoice with SLA reporting for HR and Finance.
  • Shift-based corporate menus with add-on snacks for meetings.
  • Ambassador program and co-branding kits for partners.

step 4 · go-to-market execution

30·60·90 plan

Days 0–30 · Validation

  • Launch interviews and jobs-to-be-done synthesis.
  • Secure Bonapriso and Bastos hubs, complete hygiene audits.
  • Train the concierge squad (four agents).

Days 31–60 · Pre-launch

  • Run the WhatsApp concierge MVP and analyze feedback loops.
  • Test branding and subscription site (beta).
  • Schedule six corporate tastings with target accounts.

Days 61–90 · Launch

  • Open the self-serve portal with recurring payments.
  • Deploy the “Rhythm without stress” campaign (OOH + digital).
  • Operationalize the win/loss loop with weekly reporting.

Validation KPIs to monitor

Discovery

80% of interviews validate three recurring pains around predictability and nutrition.

Concierge MVP

≥60% trial-to-paid conversion and <10% late deliveries over four weeks.

Product readiness

Net promoter score ≥35 among early subscribers; churn intent <20% at exit interviews.

Revenue traction

Monthly recurring revenue run-rate of 6M FCFA with 35% gross margin by week 12.

Each KPI ties back to research, segmentation, and positioning hypotheses and will be iterated as real usage data emerges.

Scenario projections & iteration cadence

Base case (weeks 1–12)

  • 120 household subscriptions and 6 corporate pilots by week 12.
  • Average order value: 18,500 FCFA households · 32,000 FCFA corporate.
  • Gross margin climbs from 28% to 35% as hubs reach 70% capacity.

Adjustment triggers

  • If conversion <50%, expand referral incentives and revisit pricing anchors.
  • If margins <30%, rebalance menu mix (local staples vs. premium) and renegotiate supplier terms.
  • Monthly review ritual connects research insights to product and growth experiments.

Projections are informed by market research, segmentation, and positioning assumptions, and will be recalibrated as pilot data lands.
